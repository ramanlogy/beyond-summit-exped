Overview

The World's Most Iconic Trek

The Everest Base Camp trek is Nepal's most celebrated trail — a 14-day journey that begins with a flight into Lukla airport (2,860m) and climbs steadily through one of the most dramatic landscapes on earth. You'll pass through ancient Sherpa villages, prayer-flag-draped ridges, and high alpine terrain that few humans ever witness.

Standing at 5,364 metres, surrounded by Everest, Lhotse, Nuptse and the vast white expanse of the Khumbu Glacier, is an experience that simply cannot be replicated anywhere else on earth. Our 14-day itinerary builds in proper acclimatisation days at Namche Bazaar and Dingboche, giving your body the time it needs to adapt — safely.

Every trek is led by a licensed, certified Sherpa guide whose family has lived in the Khumbu for generations. You won't just walk to Everest — you'll be introduced to it.

Day by Day

Full Itinerary

D1
Arrive Kathmandu — Briefing & Gear Check
1,400 m

Welcome to Kathmandu! Your Beyond Summit guide will meet you at the airport. The afternoon is free to explore Thamel. Evening team briefing with your lead Sherpa — we review the route, check your gear, and share weather forecasts.

Hotel Kathmandu Meals: D
D2
Fly Kathmandu → Lukla — Trek to Phakding
2,610 m

A thrilling 35-minute mountain flight into Lukla's Tenzing-Hillary Airport (2,860m) — one of the world's most dramatic runways. Trek through Cheplung and Nurning villages, descending to the Dudh Kosi river, reaching Phakding by afternoon.

Flight + Walk ~3–4 hrs Teahouse Meals: B, L, D
D3
Phakding → Namche Bazaar
3,440 m

The most dramatic day of approach. Cross suspension bridges over the roaring Dudh Kosi, pass through Monjo (the gateway to Sagarmatha National Park), then climb steeply to the Sherpa capital of Namche Bazaar — a horseshoe-shaped town with bakeries, gear shops, and the first views of Everest.

~5–6 hrs Gain: 830 m Teahouse Meals: B, L, D
D4
Namche Bazaar Acclimatisation Day
3,440 m

Rest and acclimatise. Morning hike up to Syangboche (3,800m) for panoramic Everest, Lhotse, and Ama Dablam views. Afternoon at leisure — visit the Sherpa Museum, explore the Saturday market (if timing permits), or simply rest. Pulse-ox checks with our guide each evening.

Acclimatisation Optional hike 2–3 hrs Meals: B, L, D
D5–6
Namche → Tengboche → Dingboche
4,410 m

Two days of progressively rewarding terrain. Day 5: Climb to Tengboche Monastery (3,867m) — the spiritual heart of the Khumbu, with Ama Dablam and Everest framed perfectly above the prayer halls. Day 6: Descend to the Imja Khola valley and climb to Dingboche in the wide upper valley.

2 Days ~5–6 hrs each Teahouses Meals: B, L, D
D7
Dingboche Acclimatisation — Nagarjun Hill
4,410 m

Second acclimatisation day. Morning hike to Nagarjun Hill (5,100m) for stunning views of Makalu, Lhotse, Island Peak and Ama Dablam. The extra altitude exposure prepares your body for Base Camp without sleeping high — a critical part of our safety protocol.

Acclimatisation Hike to 5,100 m Meals: B, L, D
D8–9
Dingboche → Lobuche → Gorak Shep → EBC
5,364 m

The culminating two days. Day 8: Trek to Lobuche (4,940m) along the lateral moraine of the Khumbu Glacier. Day 9: The big one — push to Gorak Shep (5,140m) and then walk the final 3.5km across glacial rubble to Everest Base Camp (5,364m). Stand where the greatest mountaineers in history began their summit pushes. Return to Gorak Shep for the night.

2 Days Summit: 5,364 m Most Memorable Day Meals: B, L, D
D10
Kala Patthar Sunrise — Descent to Pheriche
5,545 m

Pre-dawn start for Kala Patthar (5,545m) — the classic Everest photography viewpoint. Watch the summit catch first light as the sun rises over Tibet. A truly unforgettable moment. Descend rapidly to Pheriche (4,288m) as your body celebrates the lower altitude.

Summit: 5,545 m Early start 4am Meals: B, L, D
D11–13
Descend Pheriche → Namche → Lukla
2,860 m

The return is always faster — and your legs are strong. Three days descending past familiar landscapes that look completely different on the way down. Overnight in Namche (a chance to spend your remaining rupees on souvenirs) before the final push to Lukla. Celebration dinner with your Sherpa team.

3 Days ~6–7 hrs/day Meals: B, L, D
D14
Fly Lukla → Kathmandu — Farewell Dinner
1,400 m

Morning flight back to Kathmandu. Afternoon free — we recommend a massage, hot shower, and a good meal. Evening farewell dinner with your guide team. You are officially an EBC trekker — one of fewer than 40,000 who make it each year.

Flight Hotel Meals: B, D
