Southwest Ridge — the standard route from Base Camp

Ama Dablam at 6,812 metres is Nepal’s most beautiful mountain — and one of its most serious. Its name comes from Sherpa: ama (mother) and dablam (pendant amulet). The two hanging glaciers are her outstretched arms.

Technical Mountaineering — Not a Trek

Requires crampons, ice axe, and fixed rope experience. Recommended progression: Island Peak → Mera Peak → Ama Dablam.

The Southwest Ridge involves fixed rope ascenders, exposed ridge traverses, mixed rock and ice, and committed sections with no safe retreat. It is one of the most aesthetic climbing routes in the entire Himalayan range.

Route Description

A long, exposed, technically varied ridge with four distinct sections between Base Camp and the summit pyramid — one of the most aesthetic lines in all of the Himalaya.

BC
Base Camp 4,570 m
Located on the lateral moraine above the Mingbo Valley, 3–4 hours from Pangboche. Full cook and dining facilities. 5–7 days here for initial acclimatisation. Puja ceremony before any climb.
4,570 m3–4 hrs from PangbochePuja Ceremony
C1
Camp 1 — Yellow Tower 5,800 m
The Yellow Tower — a prominent orange-red rock buttress — is climbed on fixed ropes via Jumar ascenders. Above it, a narrow snow ridge leads to the C1 platform. The technical crux for many climbers.
5,800 mFixed Ropes · Jumar5–7 hrs from BC
C2
Camp 2 — Grey Tower 6,000 m
The Grey Tower is the most exposed section on the route. Exposure on both sides of the ridge is considerable. C2 accommodates 3–4 tents on a precarious platform with significant wind exposure.

Camp 3 — Below the Dablam 6,300 m
The highest camp, perched directly beneath the hanging Dablam glacier. Camp placement and approach timing are assessed by guides each season. Summit push departs at midnight.

Best Season

Autumn (Oct–Nov) is primary. Longer weather windows, ropes installed by early October. Mid-Oct to mid-Nov offers highest summit probability.

Spring (Apr–May) is quieter but has shorter weather windows. Winter and monsoon not operated.

Technical Gear List

Do not substitute trekking gear. All items required unless marked optional.

Climbing Hardware

  • 12-point crampons — step-in or hybrid
  • Ice axe — 60–70 cm general mountaineering
  • Jumar ascender — practice before expedition
  • Climbing harness — test with all layers on
  • Locking carabiners — min 4 HMS + 6 standard
  • Belay device — ATC or equivalent
  • Prussik cord — 6mm × 5m
  • Helmet — rockfall on Yellow Tower

Clothing & Footwear

  • High-altitude boots — rated to −40°C
  • Down suit or layering — 800+ fill, −30°C
  • Expedition mitts — waterproof outer + liner
  • UV goggles — not sunglasses above C1
  • Balaclava / face mask
  • Base layers ×2, mid layers ×2
  • Softshell + hardshell trousers

Camp & Personal

  • Sleeping bag — down, −30°C minimum
  • Insulated sleeping mat — inflatable
  • Headtorch — lithium + 2 spare sets
  • High-capacity power bank
  • Thermos ×2 — 1 litre each
  • Sunscreen SPF 50+
  • Personal first aid kit
Insider Knowledge

Expert Tips

Practice Your Jumar
Spend at least one full day practicing Jumar technique before arriving. The Yellow Tower rewards those who arrive prepared.
Weather Patience Wins
The summit window can be 1–3 days wide. The teams who wait correctly for weather are the ones who summit safely.
Hydration Above All
At altitude, dehydration accelerates sickness. Drink 3–4 litres per day at BC, more above 5,500m.
Trust the Rest Days
Rest days are not wasted days. Your body supercompensates during recovery. Trust the acclimatisation schedule completely.
