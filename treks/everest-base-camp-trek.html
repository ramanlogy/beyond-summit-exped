About This Trek

The World’s Most
Famous Trek

No trail on earth carries more legend. No journey at this altitude is more accessible. Everest Base Camp is the pilgrimage for those who love the mountains.

Classic Khumbu Valley — the trail to the world’s highest base camp

Everest Base Camp at 5,364 metres is the starting point of every Everest ascent — and one of the most iconic destinations on the planet. The trek follows ancient Sherpa trade routes through the Sagarmatha National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, past Buddhist monasteries, prayer flags, and yak caravans that have moved through this valley for centuries.

No Technical Skills Required

This is a trekking route, not a climbing expedition. No crampons, ropes, or prior mountaineering experience needed — only solid fitness and a willingness to go slowly.

The standard route starts with a heart-stopping mountain flight into Lukla (2,860m), then follows the Dudh Koshi River north through Phakding and up to the Sherpa capital of Namche Bazaar (3,440m). From Namche the scenery intensifies at every step: Tengboche Monastery, Dingboche's wide yak-grazed valley, the stark moraine of Lobuche, and finally Gorak Shep — the last teahouse before Base Camp.

Preparation Makes the Difference

The EBC trek requires no mountaineering skills, but demands genuine fitness. Trekkers who prepare well enjoy the journey far more. We recommend a minimum of 8–12 weeks of dedicated training before departure.

Training Recommendations

  • ▸ Weekly long hikes 3–6 hrs on hilly terrain with loaded daypack
  • ▸ Cardio 3–4x per week (running, cycling, swimming)
  • ▸ Stair climbs with weighted pack (10–12 kg)
  • ▸ Leg strength: squats, lunges, step-ups
  • ▸ Consult physician if over 50 or respiratory conditions

Altitude Sickness Prevention

  • ▸ Follow the golden rule: climb high, sleep low
  • ▸ Drink 3–4 litres of water daily at altitude
  • ▸ Consider Diamox (acetazolamide) — consult GP before trek
  • ▸ Never ascend with AMS symptoms — headache, nausea, ataxia
  • ▸ Descent is the only cure for worsening altitude sickness

Best Season

Spring (Mar–May) is the most popular. The rhododendrons bloom and Everest’s climbing season fills Base Camp with colour and excitement. April is peak season.

Autumn (Oct–Nov) offers crystal-clear post-monsoon skies and cooler temperatures — our personal favourite for visibility and photography.

Essential Gear List

Keep your pack weight under 10–12 kg. Porters carry the bulk of your kit (included). Everything on this list is available for rent in Thamel, Kathmandu.

Footwear & Clothing

  • Waterproof trekking boots — broken in before departure
  • Warm camp footwear — sandals or down booties
  • Moisture-wicking base layers ×2
  • Mid-layer fleece or softshell
  • Insulated down jacket — 800+ fill
  • Waterproof hardshell jacket & trousers
  • Warm hat, balaclava, liner gloves + outer mitts

Equipment

  • Trekking poles ×2 — strongly recommended for descent
  • UV-protective sunglasses — glacier glasses above 4,500m
  • Headtorch — essential for Kala Patthar pre-dawn
  • Daypack 25–30L — carried by you
  • Duffel bag 60–80L — carried by porter
  • Water bottles ×2 (1L each) — avoid single-use plastic
  • Gaiters — for snow above 4,500m in shoulder seasons

Health & Sleep

  • Sleeping bag — rated to −15°C minimum
  • Sleeping bag liner — adds 5–10°C extra warmth
  • Diamox (acetazolamide) — consult GP, get prescription before trek
  • Ibuprofen, rehydration salts, blister kit
  • SPF 50+ sunscreen & lip balm
  • High-capacity power bank — teahouses charge for electricity
  • Offline maps downloaded (Maps.me / Gaia GPS)
Insider Knowledge

Expert Tips

Go Slow, Go Far
Your pace should be "pressure breathing slow" — slower than you think is necessary. The trekkers who rush are the ones who turn around at Lobuche.
Build in Buffer Days
Lukla flights are weather-dependent. Never book an international connection within 48 hours of your scheduled Lukla flight. This is the single biggest mistake first-time trekkers make.
Drink More Than You Think
At altitude, dehydration accelerates AMS symptoms dramatically. Aim for 3–4 litres per day. Buy boiled or treated water from teahouses — carry a Steripen or purification tablets.
Trust Your Sherpa
Your Sherpa guide has forgotten more about altitude and this route than any guidebook contains. If they say rest, rest. If they say turnaround, turnaround. They are right.
