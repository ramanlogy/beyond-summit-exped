Trekking Guide 2026

Nepal’s MostBeloved Trek

Standing inside the Annapurna Sanctuary — a natural amphitheatre of 8,000-metre peaks — is unlike anywhere else on earth. This guide covers everything you need to plan your trek.

The Annapurna Sanctuary — ringed by six peaks over 6,000m

If the Everest Base Camp trek is Nepal’s most famous trail, the Annapurna Base Camp trek is its most beloved. Where EBC offers raw high-altitude drama and mountaineering history, the ABC trek delivers something rarer: the feeling of being completely enclosed within a circle of Himalayan giants.

Annapurna Base Camp sits at 4,130 metres at the heart of the Annapurna Sanctuary — a high glacial basin ringed by some of the world’s most dramatic peaks, including Annapurna I (8,091m), the tenth-highest mountain on Earth and the first 8,000-metre peak ever climbed by humans.

Since April 2023 — Licensed Guide Mandatory

Nepal requires all trekkers in national parks and conservation areas to be accompanied by a licensed guide. Beyond Summit guides are TIMS-registered, English-speaking, and trained in wilderness first aid.

The route passes through Nepal’s most diverse terrain — terraced rice paddies, dense rhododendron forests, traditional Gurung villages, and glacial moraines — all within a single trek. It is shorter and lower in maximum altitude than EBC, making it more accessible to a broader range of trekkers.

Spring rhododendron in bloom — March to May
Ghandruk — Nepal’s finest Gurung village
⚖️

Deciding between Annapurna Base Camp and Everest Base Camp? ABC is lower (4,130m vs 5,364m), shorter (12 days vs 14 days), and more affordable. ABC is the better first Himalayan trek; EBC is the natural next step.

Avalanche Risk — Sanctuary Gorge

The gorge between Deurali and MBC passes beneath slopes that can avalanche after heavy snowfall. Beyond Summit monitors Sanctuary weather from Pokhara before and during every trek. If conditions require an extra day in Chhomrong, that call is made without hesitation.

Trek Itinerary

This is Beyond Summit’s standard 12-day framework from Pokhara. It incorporates a rest day at Chhomrong and cultural half-day in Ghandruk — resulting in better acclimatisation and a higher completion rate than cut-price 10-day versions.

Costs & Permits

Permits Required (2026)

Two permits are required and must be carried throughout as they are checked at multiple checkpoints along the trail.

  1. ACAP — Annapurna Conservation Area Project Permit — NPR 3,000 (~USD 22) per person. Permit fees fund conservation and local community projects.
  2. TIMS Card (Trekkers’ Information Management System) — NPR 2,000 (~USD 15) for trekkers booking through a licensed agency. Mandatory for all trekkers in Nepal.
Permits Included with Beyond Summit

When trekking with Beyond Summit, both your ACAP permit and TIMS card are arranged before departure. No queuing at permit offices — our pre-trek briefing covers collection, gear check, and full route orientation.

Gear & Fitness
What to Bring

Essential Gear List

The ABC trek reaches 4,130m — cold at night but not extreme by Himalayan standards. Keep your pack weight under 8–10kg; porters carry the main duffel bag on Beyond Summit packages. Note that teahouse prices above 3,000m increase significantly — carry sufficient Nepali rupees as ATMs are not available above Chhomrong.

Footwear & Clothing

  • Trekking boots — waterproof, ankle-support, broken in
  • Gaiters — for monsoon or winter season
  • Base layers ×2 — moisture-wicking
  • Mid-layer fleece
  • Insulated jacket — down or synthetic
  • Hardshell jacket & trousers — waterproof
  • Warm hat, buff, liner gloves
  • UV sunglasses & sunscreen SPF 50+

Camp & Sleep

  • Sleeping bag — down, −10°C rated minimum
  • Trekking poles — non-negotiable for stone steps
  • Headtorch — with spare batteries
  • Power bank — electricity charged above Chhomrong
  • Water bottles ×2 — 1 litre each
  • Water purification — tablets or filter
  • Thermos — for summit morning hot drinks

Health & First Aid

  • Ibuprofen & paracetamol
  • Diamox (Acetazolamide) — consult GP; optional at 4,130m
  • Oral rehydration salts
  • Blister kit — Compeed, moleskin
  • Insect repellent — lower elevations in spring
  • Knee strapping tape — for descent
  • Pulse oximeter (optional; guides carry one)

Fitness Requirements

The ABC trek is graded moderate, making it one of the most accessible Himalayan trekking experiences. However, “moderate” means 6–7 hours of walking per day on steep, often uneven terrain. The toughest day — the climb from Tikhedhunga to Ghorepani — involves over 1,300 metres of ascent on stone staircases.

We recommend a minimum of 6–8 weeks of preparation including regular hikes of 3–5 hours with 500–800m of elevation gain, cardiovascular training 3 times per week, and step or stair climbing with a loaded pack.

Insider Knowledge

Expert Tips

Spring = Rhododendrons
Mid-March through April the forest between Ghandruk and Chhomrong erupts in crimson and pink. April is our top recommendation for the full ABC experience.
Hydrate Constantly
At altitude, dehydration accelerates sickness. Drink 3–4 litres per day. Carry purification tablets as bottled water generates plastic waste above Chhomrong.
Take Chhomrong Day
The acclimatisation rest day in Chhomrong is not a luxury — it’s what separates successful Sanctuary treks from turnarounds above Deurali.
Trekking Poles Essential
The stone staircase descent from Chhomrong to Nayapul is notoriously hard on knees. Bring or rent quality poles in Pokhara before departure — they are not optional on this route.
