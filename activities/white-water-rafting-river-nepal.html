About Rafting in Nepal

Nepal’s Rivers Are
World-Class

Himalayan snowmelt carved some of the planet's most dramatic gorges. Now they offer whitewater experiences you simply cannot find anywhere else on earth.

Kali Gandaki Gorge — deepest on earth, best run October through December

Nepal has more whitewater per square kilometre than almost any country on earth. The rivers here descend from over 8,000 metres to sea level in a matter of hundreds of kilometres — creating gradient, power, and character in the water that nothing else replicates. From the gentle, family-friendly Trishuli day trip to the demanding ten-day Sun Koshi expedition through one of Asia's last true wilderness corridors, every experience level has a river waiting.

No Rafting Experience Required

Our half-day and one-day Trishuli trips are open to complete beginners from age 12. Multi-day river expeditions require basic swimming ability. We provide all safety equipment, certified guides and comprehensive pre-trip briefings.

Beyond Summit's rafting guides hold internationally certified swift-water rescue qualifications and carry full first-aid kits, throw bags, and satellite communication on all multi-day expeditions. Safety is the only non-negotiable.

The classic Nepal introduction. The Trishuli runs alongside the Prithvi Highway, making logistics smooth and the day-trip experience accessible from Kathmandu or Pokhara. Bhote Koshi

Nepal's steepest commercial rafting river. The Bhote Koshi drops 37 metres per kilometre through a narrow granite gorge near the Tibet border — twice the gradient of the Colorado through the Grand Canyon. Two days, continuous Class IV–V, riverside camp. Not for the faint-hearted. Absolutely unforgettable.

Kali Gandaki

The Kali Gandaki flows through the world's deepest river gorge — flanked by Annapurna and Dhaulagiri, both over 8,000 metres. A three-to-four-day run combining good whitewater with extraordinary landscape drama. Often combined with the Annapurna Circuit or Upper Mustang trek for a complete Nepal adventure.

International Whitewater Grading Scale

The International Scale of River Difficulty classifies rapids from Grade I (flatwater) to Grade VI (generally unrunnable). We operate Grade II through Grade V commercially. Your Beyond Summit guide will always assess live conditions on the day — river grades change with water level.

GradeClassificationDescriptionBSE RiverSuitable For
Grade II Beginner Easy rapids with small waves. Obvious clear channels. Requires basic paddle strokes. Seti River All ages 8+. No experience needed.
Grade III Intermediate Moderate rapids with irregular waves. Some maneuvering required. Swim recovery straightforward. Trishuli, Kali Gandaki Age 12+. No experience needed. Good fitness.
Grade IV Advanced Powerful rapids demanding precise maneuvering. Long swims possible. Scout ahead required. Kali Gandaki upper, Sun Koshi Age 14–16+. Basic swim confidence. Some prior rafting helpful.
Grade V Expert Extremely difficult. Long violent rapids, steep drops, limited recovery zones. Serious hazard. Bhote Koshi Age 18+. Strong swimmer essential. Prior Class IV experience required.
What to Bring on Your Rafting Day

We provide all safety equipment. Bring what keeps you comfortable on and off the water. Pack light — everything on the river either gets wet or goes into a dry bag.

Clothing

  • Swimwear under everything — you will get wet
  • Quick-dry shorts or leggings
  • Synthetic or wool t-shirt — no cotton
  • Light fleece (Oct–Mar, mornings)
  • Sandals with strap or old trainers
  • Flip-flops or dry shoes for camp (multi-day)

Personal Items

  • Sunscreen SPF 50+ — water reflects UV intensely
  • Sunglasses with strap — they will fly off
  • Lip balm SPF
  • Water bottle (we provide refills)
  • Energy snacks for long sections
  • Small towel for after the river
  • Change of dry clothes in your vehicle bag

Multi-Day Extras

  • Sleeping bag (down, 5–10°C rating)
  • Headtorch — beach camps have no power
  • Insect repellent (terai section)
  • Small personal dry bag for valuables
  • Book or cards — evenings on the beach are long and quiet
  • Waterproof camera or phone case

Best Seasons

October–December is ideal — post-monsoon flows are powerful and consistent, skies are clear, temperatures warm.

March–May is excellent — lower water, technical rapids, spring heat pleasant.

July–August: Monsoon flood conditions. Most rivers closed. Bhote Koshi remains open for expert-only runs with modified itineraries.

Safety Standards

From Our River Guides

Insider Tips

Start Early
Himalayan rivers warm up fast. Start by 7am on summer days — you'll have the gorge to yourself and avoid afternoon winds.
Listen to Your Guide
Our guides read the water every single day. When they say lean left, lean left immediately — there's always a reason.
Respect the Water Level
After rain, rivers can rise metres in hours. We check levels every morning and will reschedule if conditions exceed client capability.
Waterproof Your Camera
The best river shots come from the raft. A cheap waterproof case is worth 50 times its cost. We won't stop for you to fish your phone out of the Bhote Koshi.
